# LinkedIn Post Repurposing Guide

This guide teaches agents how to transform long-form content (blogs, video transcripts) into high-performing LinkedIn posts. Every framework, formula, and example below is derived from patterns observed in viral LinkedIn content.

---

## Post Architecture

Every viral LinkedIn post follows this skeleton:

```
Lines 1-2  → HOOK (get the "see more" click)
[blank line]
Lines 3-5  → STORY / CONTEXT (build relatability)
[blank line]
Body       → VALUE (deliver the insight)
[blank line]
Closing    → TAKEAWAY + CTA (drive engagement)
[blank line]
Hashtags   → 3-5 relevant tags
```

### Optimal Post Length
- **Sweet spot: 800–1,300 characters** (~150-250 words)
- Under 500 characters = "low effort" → 35% lower engagement
- Over 2,000 characters = diminishing returns unless formatting is exceptional
- LinkedIn maximum: 3,000 characters

---

## The "See More" Rule

This is the single most important concept for LinkedIn posts.

### Critical Numbers
- **Desktop**: Only ~210 characters visible before "see more"
- **Mobile**: Only ~140 characters visible before "see more"
- **60-70% of readers will NEVER click "see more"** if the hook isn't compelling

### Strategy
- Treat the first 2-3 lines as your **headline**
- Front-load your most compelling value
- Create an "open loop" — raise a question that can only be answered by reading the rest
- **NEVER** waste this space with greetings, pleasantries, or filler
- Spend **50% of your editing time** on perfecting the hook

## Step-by-Step Repurposing Process

### Step 1: Extract the Core Insight

From the source content, identify:
- The **single most powerful takeaway** (becomes the hook)
- **3-5 supporting points** (become the body)
- **1 personal angle or story** (makes it relatable)
- **1 actionable lesson** (becomes the closing)

### Step 2: Pick a Repurposing Strategy

| Blog Content Type | LinkedIn Format |
|:---|:---|
| Main argument / thesis | Counterintuitive hook + story post |
| Step-by-step guide | Numbered listicle post |
| Statistics / data | "Surprising stat" hook + breakdown |
| Key quotes | Hook-centered short post |
| Personal experience | Story-driven narrative post |

### Step 3: Rewrite for LinkedIn Tone

| ❌ Blog Tone | ✅ LinkedIn Tone |
|:---|:---|
| "According to research, 73% of..." | "73% of managers never receive formal training. And it shows." |
| "In this article, we will explore..." | "Nobody talks about this, but..." |
| "It can be concluded that..." | "Here's the uncomfortable truth:" |
| "Users should consider implementing..." | "Try this instead:" |

### Step 4: Format for Mobile

- **One sentence per line** — the single most important formatting rule
- **Blank lines between thoughts** — creates breathing room
- **Short sentences** — under 12 words performs best on mobile
- **Maximum 2 sentences per block**
- Over 70% of LinkedIn is consumed on mobile — format accordingly

### Step 5: Add the CTA and Hashtags

- End with a question to drive comments (comments are weighted 5-15x more than likes)
- Place 3-5 hashtags at the very end, separated by a blank line
- **NEVER put external links in the post body** — the algorithm heavily penalizes this

---

## The External Link Rule

This is the most emphasized finding across all research:

> **LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses posts with external links in the body.**

If you need to link to the original blog/article:
- Post the content natively on LinkedIn
- Add the link in the **first comment**
- In the post, say: "Link in comments 👇" or "Full article in my first comment"

## Hook Formulas

### 1. The Counterintuitive Statement
Challenge conventional wisdom to create cognitive dissonance.

```
Template: "[Common advice] is actually hurting your [goal]."

Example: "Follow your passion" is the worst career advice I ever received.
```

### 2. The Personal Confession / Vulnerability
Share a personal failure or struggle.

```
Template: "I [failed/struggled with something]. Here's what I learned."

Example: I got fired from my dream job last Tuesday.
Best thing that ever happened to me.
```

### 3. The Surprising Statistic
Lead with a number that shocks.

```
Template: "[Shocking stat]. Here's what it means for [audience]."

Example: 73% of managers have never received any formal leadership training.
And it shows.
```

### 4. The Bold Declaration
Make a strong, opinionated statement.

```
Template: "[Strong opinion about industry/topic]."

Example: Your resume doesn't get you hired.
Your network does.
```

### 5. The Achievement Hook
Share a result or accomplishment.

```
Template: "I [achieved result] in [timeframe]. Here's my exact process:"

Example: I grew my team from 3 to 30 in 18 months without a single bad hire.
Here's my exact process:
```

### 6. The "I was..." Hook
Vulnerability + transformation.

```
Template: "I was [struggling state]. Then [turning point]. Now [result]."

Example: I was wrong about remote work.
Here's what changed my mind.
```

### 7. The "Stop doing..." Hook
Authoritative and direct.

```
Template: "Stop [common action]. It's killing your [goal]. Do this instead:"

Example: Stop saying "I'm passionate about..." in your LinkedIn headline.
Do this instead:
```

## Post Formulas

### Formula 1: The "Hard Truth" Post
Best for thought leadership.

```
Unpopular opinion: [Industry belief] is overrated.

I've seen people with [X] fail because of [Y].

What actually works:

→ [Point 1]
→ [Point 2]
→ [Point 3]

Learned this the hard way.

What's your take — agree or disagree? 👇

#Leadership #CareerAdvice #ProfessionalGrowth
```

### Formula 2: The "Mini-Story" Post
Best for personal branding.

```
Last year I [struggled with X].

Then one [person/event] told me [insight].

I fixed [Y] and got [Z result].

Here's what changed:

→ [Lesson 1]
→ [Lesson 2]
→ [Lesson 3]

Sometimes it's not your skills. It's your [presentation/mindset/approach].

Has this happened to you? I'd love to hear your story 👇

#ProfessionalDevelopment #Startup
```

### Formula 3: The "Breakdown" Post
Best for tactical/educational content.

```
My [project/strategy] brought me [specific result].

Here's what I optimized:

1. [Tip with brief explanation]
2. [Tip with brief explanation]
3. [Tip with brief explanation]
4. [Tip with brief explanation]
5. [Tip with brief explanation]

Most people ignore #3. Which one are you fixing first?

#Marketing #GrowthHacking #SaaS
```

### Formula 4: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
Best for solution-oriented posts.

```
Struggling to get consistent leads on LinkedIn?

It's frustrating to spend hours crafting posts that get zero engagement.

You try everything — hashtags, posting times, engagement pods.
Nothing works.

That's because most advice misses the real problem.

Here's what actually moves the needle:

→ [Solution point 1]
→ [Solution point 2]
→ [Solution point 3]

What's been your biggest struggle with LinkedIn content? 👇

#ContentMarketing #LinkedInTips #B2B
```

### Formula 5: SLA (Story-Lesson-Application)
Best for concise, high-impact posts.

```
[Brief professional/personal story — under 6 sentences]

The lesson:
[One-sentence core takeaway]

3 things you can do today:

1. [Actionable step]
2. [Actionable step]
3. [Actionable step]

Which one will you try first? 👇

#CareerGrowth #Leadership
```

## Formatting Rules

### Bullet Points
Use these symbols instead of standard dashes:
```
→ Arrow points (preferred for steps/flow)
• Bullet dots (for lists)
✅ Checkmarks (for do's/tips)
❌ Crosses (for don'ts/mistakes)
```

### Emojis
- **1-3 per post maximum** — used as visual anchors, not decoration
- Place at the start of sections or beside key points
- Never use emojis in every line — this reads as spammy
- Good: 🔑 💡 📊 → (functional emojis)
- Bad: 🎉🔥🚀💪🙏 (decorative emoji walls)

### Bold and Emphasis
- LinkedIn doesn't support standard markdown bold
- Use CAPS sparingly for emphasis on key words
- Or use Unicode bold characters for critical phrases

### Line Spacing
```
Good:
I learned 3 things.

First, consistency matters.

Second, quality beats quantity.

Third, engagement is a two-way street.

---

Bad:
I learned 3 things. First, consistency matters. Second, quality beats quantity. Third, engagement is a two-way street.
```

## Hashtag Strategy

### Rules
- **3-5 hashtags maximum** — more looks spammy
- Place at the **very end** of the post, after a blank line
- Never start a post with a hashtag

### Mix Strategy
- **1-2 broad tags**: #Leadership, #Marketing, #Technology
- **2-3 niche tags**: #SaaSGrowth, #RemoteWorkTips, #DevOps
- **Avoid overused tags**: #motivation, #success, #hustle (too saturated)

## Complete Post Examples

### Example 1: Blog about "5 Productivity Systems" → LinkedIn Post

```
I tested 5 popular productivity systems for 6 months.

Only 1 actually stuck.

Here's the uncomfortable truth about productivity advice:

Most systems fail because they fight your natural energy patterns.

Time blocking? Great in theory.
Pomodoro? Works for shallow tasks.
GTD? Too much overhead.

The one that worked:

→ Energy mapping: track your focus levels for 1 week
→ Batch similar tasks during your peak hours
→ Protect your first 2 hours — no meetings, no Slack
→ Let your low-energy hours handle admin

The result: 40% more deep work with zero extra hours.

Stop copying someone else's system.
Build one that matches YOUR brain.

Which productivity method has actually worked for you? 👇

#Productivity #TimeManagement #RemoteWork
```

### Example 2: YouTube Video Transcript (Dev Talk) → LinkedIn Post

```
I watched a mass migration from REST to GraphQL fail spectacularly.

The team converted 200+ endpoints in a single sprint.

No gradual rollout. No fallback. No safety net.

Production broke for 6 hours on launch day.

Here's what they should have done:

→ Run both APIs in parallel during transition
→ Migrate one service at a time, not all at once
→ Implement DataLoader for N+1 queries BEFORE going live
→ Set up monitoring specifically for GraphQL query depth

GraphQL is powerful.
But it's a new architecture, not a format swap.

Treat it that way and you'll avoid the pain.

Have you been through a painful tech migration? What went wrong? 👇

#Engineering #GraphQL #SoftwareArchitecture #WebDev
```

## Engagement Amplification

### The "Golden Hour" Rule
- The first 60-90 minutes after posting are critical
- LinkedIn tests your post with a small audience first
- Strong early engagement (especially comments) triggers wider distribution
- **Implication**: Generate posts that are designed to provoke immediate comments

### Algorithm Signal Hierarchy
| Signal | Weight | What Drives It |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Comments (15+ words) | **Highest** (5-15x likes) | Questions, controversial takes, invitations to share |
| Shares with commentary | **High** (2-4x likes) | Educational content that makes the sharer look smart |
| Dwell time | **High** | Long, well-formatted posts that hold attention |
| Likes/Reactions | **Baseline** (1x) | Quick, thumb-stopping content |

### Content That Drives Each Type
- **Comments**: Questions, contrarian takes, "which one?" prompts
- **Shares**: Data, frameworks, educational resources
- **Dwell time**: Stories, well-spaced formatting, hooks with open loops

## Quality Checklist

Before outputting a LinkedIn post, verify:

- [ ] Hook is under 210 characters and creates an open loop
- [ ] Post is 800-1,300 characters total
- [ ] One sentence per line — no walls of text
- [ ] Blank lines between every thought block
- [ ] Tone is conversational, not corporate/formal
- [ ] No external links in the post body
- [ ] Emojis used sparingly (1-3 max) as visual anchors
- [ ] Bullets use → or • instead of markdown dashes
- [ ] Ends with a specific question to drive comments
- [ ] 3-5 hashtags at the end (mix of broad + niche)
- [ ] Content provides standalone value without clicking away
- [ ] Reads well on mobile (short lines, frequent breaks)
